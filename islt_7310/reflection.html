Past

I began my work experience in customer service with the Missouri Department of Revenue. I answered correspondence and worked with taxpayers visiting the office to resolve past-due balances. I learned quickly the ability to deliver information in a clear, positive manner is a skill.

I've worked in Information Technology for over 20 years and have experience helping users make their day-to-day activities more efficient and increase accuracy. After all of the various roles I've filled, I have decided that the two things I love most about my job are working with customers to develop a system to make their lives easier and following up afterward to ensure the system meets their needs and looking for ways to improve it even more.

Present

I take the approach of teamwork. I encourage working together to resolve an issue. No one should ever feel alone, without someone to turn to for guidance or a different perspective. My friends and family often tease me for my empathetic approach to life. I often make up a story about a person I encounter to help me remember I may not be seeing them at their best. A driver cut me off because they were rushing home to a sick child. The waitress wasn't providing optimal service because her boyfriend just broke it off. Using this approach in my daily life allows me to remember we are all humans and face unseen and unspoken challenges. We all need to treat one another with respect and care. We need to work together for the greater good of our society.

Future

With my background in customer service, experience in volunteer training, and knowledge of developing web applications, professional development is a logical next step. I enjoy the challenge of making difficult tasks easy and breaking big projects down into manageable chunks for simple execution. I look forward to this new opportunity in my career.
